# Unit 1: My Family and Me

## Chapter 1: Two Little Hands

### Let us sing

Two little hands
go clap, clap, clap.
Two little legs
go tap, tap, tap.
Two little eyes
are open wide.
One little head
goes side to side.

Two little eyes to look around.
Two little ears to hear any sound.
One little nose to smell and breathe.
One little mouth that likes to eat.
Hands to clap, legs to walk
Nose to smell, mouth to talk
Head to move, eyes to see
I have a little body that belongs to me.

* Now circle ‘One, two, to’ in the song.

**Sight words**
one | to

**New words**
hand | leg | head | eye | ear | nose | mouth

* Use different combinations and variations for the activity.

## Chapter 2: The Cap-seller and the Monkeys

### Let us read

**The Cap-seller and the Monkeys**

A cap-seller was passing through a jungle. He was very tired. He sat under a tree and put his basket of caps beside him. Then he lay down and fell asleep.

When he woke up, he saw that all his caps were gone. He looked up and saw some monkeys sitting on the branches of the tree. Each monkey had a cap on its head.

The cap-seller was very angry. He shook his fist at the monkeys and shouted, “Give me back my caps!”

The monkeys only made faces at him. The cap-seller thought for a while. Then he took off his own cap and threw it on the ground.

The monkeys did the same. The cap-seller quickly picked up all the caps and put them back in his basket. Then he went on his way, happy and content.

### Note to the teacher
* Read the story aloud to the children.
* Encourage children to enact the story.

### Let us speak

**A. Answer the questions**

1. Where did the cap-seller sit?
2. What did he see when he woke up?
3. What did the cap-seller do to get his caps back?

### Let us read

**Caps**
